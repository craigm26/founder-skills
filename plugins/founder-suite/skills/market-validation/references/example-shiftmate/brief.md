# ShiftMate — Build & Integrate Brief

*Illustrative example. Date: June 2, 2026 · Source: a genericized market-evidence pack (18 illustrative claims, 8 competitors). ShiftMate is a fictional product used to demonstrate the `market-validation` skill against the real, public shift-scheduling market.*

## Verdict recap
There is a real, well-funded market for helping hourly teams handle shift coverage — but the category is **crowded with full-suite incumbents and strong free tiers**, so the opportunity is a **sharp wedge**, not another general scheduler.

Confidence: HIGH the pain & market are real · MODERATE that a standalone swap app is the right wedge · LOW that free-tier incumbents leave durable room to charge for swapping alone.

## Recommended product & wedge
Build for the **swap / open-shift marketplace** where full-suite tools are weakest, and pick a **beachhead vertical** (e.g., quick-service restaurants or retail) rather than competing as a general scheduler. Monetize on **filled-shift value or a per-location floor** — not a thin per-user add-on that the free tier undercuts.

## Why this wins
- **Demand is proven and paid:** operators already budget per-employee-per-month for scheduling, and carry the salary cost of manual coverage.
- **Incumbents are weakest at liquidity:** swap features exist but are clunky; a true marketplace with fast matching is a differentiated wedge.
- **Compliance is a moat seed:** predictive-scheduling rules make a documented system-of-record more valuable than a text thread.

## Risks to design against
- **Feature, not product:** swap-only is easy to copy — become the system of record, not a bolt-on.
- **Zero-price gravity:** a capable free scheduler + group texts anchor WTP low; attach price to value the free path can't deliver.
- **Crowded GTM:** differentiate on a vertical and on marketplace liquidity, not on being "another scheduler."
